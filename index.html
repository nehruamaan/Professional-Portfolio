How I think.

I don't view Product, Strategy, and GTM as separate. I treat them as a continuous execution loop, from chaos to scale.

01
Phase 1 · Consultant
Structuring the Chaos
When dropped into ambiguity, I don't wait for perfect data. I use hypothesis-driven problem solving and MECE issue trees to break large business problems into actionable, testable components.
MECE Decomposition Hypothesis Trees Stakeholder Mapping
02
Phase 2 · Product Manager
Ruthless Focus
Once the problem is structured, I shift from divergent exploration to convergent execution. I use the Double Diamond to narrow scope and RICE scoring to prioritize the highest-leverage interventions.
Double Diamond RICE Scoring User Behavior Analysis
03
Phase 3 · GTM Strategist
Scaling the Engine
A great product fails without distribution. I design with the growth flywheel in mind ensuring user acquisition, product experience and retention form a repeatable, self-sustaining loop.
Growth Flywheel GTM Sequencing KPI / OKR Design
The Product Strategy Canvas
Consulting × Product: Hypothesis-driven thinking ensures engineering resources aren't wasted on low-value problems.
Product × GTM: Features are built with distribution in mind, designed to trigger product-led growth from day one.
GTM × Consulting: Market friction is often an information problem before it becomes a sales problem. Understanding it can reshape business strategy.
